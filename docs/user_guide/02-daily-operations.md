# 2. Daily Operations

Before opening your shop to customers, there are a few essential daily tasks to ensure your billing is accurate and up-to-date. This section covers setting the daily metal rates, adjusting making charges, and reading your daily dashboard summary.

## Setting Daily Metal Rates

Gold and Silver rates fluctuate daily. JewelloSoft makes it incredibly simple to update these rates so that every invoice generated that day uses the correct, current market value.

1. Navigate to the **Settings** menu via the left sidebar.
2. Select the **Rates** tab.
3. Locate the **Today's Rates** section.
4. **Gold Rate (per 10g)**: Enter the current 22K or 24K market rate for 10 grams of Gold.
5. **Silver Rate (per 10g)**: Enter the current market rate for 10 grams of Silver.
6. Click **Save Changes**. 

> **Tip**: The top navigation bar will instantly update to reflect these new rates, ensuring your staff is always aware of the current pricing.

## Configuring Making Charges

JewelloSoft allows you to set default making charges that will automatically apply to jewelry items during the billing process, speeding up checkout times.

1. In the same **Rates** section under **Settings**, locate the **Making Charges** panel.
2. You can define making charges in two ways:
   - **Flat Rate per Gram (₹)**: A fixed currency amount charged per gram of the item's weight.
   - **Percentage (%)**: A percentage of the item's total metal value.
3. Set your shop's standard policy and click **Save Changes**. 

*Note: You can always override these default making charges manually on individual items during the billing process.*

## The Dashboard Overview

The **Dashboard** is the first screen you see after logging in. It provides a real-time snapshot of your business health.

- **Today's Sales**: Displays the total revenue generated from all invoices created today.
- **Recent Invoices**: A quick list of the most recent transactions. You can click on any of these to reprint the receipt or view the details.
- **Low Stock Alerts**: If any items in your inventory have fallen below their recommended threshold, they will be highlighted here so you know what needs to be restocked.
- **Daily Summary Chart**: A visual representation of your sales performance over the current week or month.

By keeping your rates updated and monitoring the dashboard, you can maintain precise control over your daily retail operations. Next, learn how to process customer transactions in the **[Billing & Invoicing](./03-billing-and-invoicing.md)** guide.
